## User Summary

## The Shift from Manual Proof to Push-Authorization
While the traditional 'screenshot and upload' method (Proof of Transfer) remains a staple for small businesses in Costa Rica, it is becoming a **security liability rather than a convenience**. The rise of sophisticated 'fake voucher' applications has forced a shift in the merchant-customer trust dynamic; businesses are moving toward **'Solicitud de Pago' (Request for Payment)**. This non-obvious transition replaces the customer-led transfer with a merchant-initiated request that triggers a push notification in the user’s banking app. This eliminates the friction of switching apps and, more importantly, provides **instant cryptographic verification** that renders screenshots obsolete.

## The Professionalization of the SINPE Ecosystem
There is an emerging divide between 'informal' SINPE Móvil usage and the 'professionalized' SINPE IBAN integrations. High-value e-commerce is bypassing the ₡200,000 limits of mobile-linked accounts by using **dynamic QR codes** that bridge the gap between ease-of-use and bank-grade security. > **The critical insight for 2026 is that SINPE is no longer just a 'payment method' but a backend infrastructure** that local gateways like Tilopay and GreenPay are now automating. This means that for the first time, local businesses can achieve *real-time settlement* without the 3-5% commission fees associated with traditional credit card processors, fundamentally altering the profit margins of Costa Rican digital trade.

## Combatting the 'Verification Lag'
As SINPE evolves, the 'Verification Lag'—the time between a customer sending a payment and a merchant manually confirming it—has become the primary bottleneck for local e-commerce. To solve this, developers are increasingly using **OCR (Optical Character Recognition) and API hooks** to scrape SMS notifications or bank feeds. This *semi-automation* allows small merchants to scale their operations without hiring dedicated staff just to check bank accounts, effectively turning a peer-to-peer tool into a robust enterprise resource planning (ERP) component.

## Detailed Findings

In 2026, the payment landscape in Costa Rica is dominated by the SINPE (Sistema Nacional de Pagos Electrónicos) ecosystem, primarily split between SINPE Móvil (P2P/Small Business) and SINPE IBAN (B2B/High-value). SINPE Móvil operates via phone numbers and typically involves a manual 'Proof of Transfer Upload' workflow where customers provide screenshots of bank vouchers. This method is popular due to its zero-fee structure for transactions under certain daily limits (₡100,000–₡200,000). However, it faces security challenges like fake voucher apps, leading merchants to verify funds directly in bank accounts before order fulfillment. SINPE IBAN is used for larger transactions and is increasingly integrated into payment gateways like Tilopay and PixelPay. A significant advancement is the 'Solicitud de Pago' (Request for Payment) feature by the BCCR, which allows merchants to push payment requests directly to a customer's banking app for approval, bypassing the need for manual screenshots. Furthermore, SINPE QR codes are becoming a standard for dynamic checkout, pre-populating payment data to reduce errors and fraud.
